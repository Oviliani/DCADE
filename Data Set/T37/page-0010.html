PERSONAL:
  • Valdes is a cousin of Carlos Beltran.
  • Juan signed with Indians scout Henry Cruz on June 17, 2003 after the Tribe drafted him in the 5th round of the draft out of Fernando Callejo High School in Manati, Puerto Rico.
  • Before 2005 spring training, the Baseball America Prospect Handbook rated Valdes as 13th-best prospect in the Indians organization.
  • Juan is a good student of the game. He watches the pitcher's tendencies -- what the pitcher throws in various situations -- remembers them and applies that knowledge. (And that is the definition of wisdom: knowledge applied).
  • August 24, 2007: Valdes was suspended 50 games for using a performance-enhancing substance.
BATTING:
  • Valdes is a leadoff hitter. He gets on base because he is patient, so that he will accept a walk. And he can bunt his way on.

    He has been switch-hitting since he was 14 years old. And Juan finishes his smooth, level swing with both hands on the bat all the way through the strike zone. He makes more hard contact from the right side.
  • Juan is a slap hitter who needs to make good, consistent contact and improve his discipline at the plate to stay a leadoff hitter. He has good pitch recognition.
FIELDING:
  • Juan is a good centerfielder. He gets a great jump on balls. Not very many center fielders cover as much ground.
  • Valdes doesn't have much of an arm, so right field is not within his capability.
RUNNING:
  • Valdes is a fast runner. He has pure speed to steal bases with.

PERSONAL:
  • Jorge originally signed with the Diamondbacks and scout Oswaldo Alvarez. But after the team stopped its working agreement with Monterrey of the Mexican League in a cost-cutting measure after the 2000 season, Monterrey pulled De la Rosa back and sold his contract to the highest bidder.

    Boston beat out the Braves, Dodgers, Giants, and Yankees with a bonus in the $600,000 range. (Monterrey got 60 percent of that money; Jorge got 40 percent.) Then-Red Sox G.M. Dan Duquette dubbed de la Rosa "the Mexican John Rocker."
  • De la Rosa was chosen for the 2003 All Star Futures Game, played on July 13 at U.S. Cellular Field in Chicago as part of the All Star Game festivities.
  • Before 2005 spring training, the Baseball America Prospect Handbook rated Jorge as the 13th-best prospect in the Brewers organization.
  • In 2006, De La Rosa pitched for Team Mexico in the World Baseball Classic. In three games, De La Rosa pitched 3 1/3 innings and allowed no runs, two hits, and two walks. He struck out three batters, including Ken Griffey, Jr.
  • De La Rosa, after having unsuccessful stops with Milwaukee and Kansas City, worked extensively with Ron Svetich, the Rockies’ mental skills coach. Among other things, Svetich impressed upon De La Rosa the importance of taking deep breaths and counting to 10 to relax when he gets in trouble rather than to try to bull his way onward and, more often than not, compound his difficulties.
“I’ve learned more, I control myself, I throw more quality strikes and nothing bothers me when I’m out there,” De La Rosa said. (May 2009)

TRANSACTION REPORT
  • November 28, 2003: The Diamondbacks sent P Curt Schilling to the Red Sox, acquiring De la Rosa, P Casey Fossum, P Brandon Lyon, and OF Michael Goss.

    But Jorge never played in the D'Backs organization, because three days later . . .
  • December 1, 2003: The Brewers sent slugger Richie Sexson, P Shane Nance and OF Noochie Varner to the Diamondbacks, acquiring De la Rosa, SS Craig Cousell, 2B Junior Spivey, 1B Lyle Overbay, C Chad Moeller, and P Chris Capuano.
  • July 25, 2006: The Royals sent INF Tony Graffanino to the Brewers, acquiring De La Rosa.
  • January 18, 2008: Jorge and the Royals avoided salary arbitration, agreeing to a $1.025 million contract plus performance bonuses.
  • May 1, 2008: De La Rosa was acquired by the Rockies as the player to be named that sent RHP Ramon Ramirez to the Royals at the end of March. The Royals are believed to have sent roughly $200,000 with De La Rosa to the Rockies, an amount that approximates the remaining difference in salaries between De La Rosa and Ramirez.
  • January 20, 2009: Jorge and the Rockies avoided salary arbitration, agreeing to a one-year, $2 million contract.
  • January 15, 2010: De La Rosa and the Rockies again avoided arbitration when he signed a one-year $5.6 million contract, with another $300,000 if he reaches 220 innings pitched in 2010. (Editor's note: He pitched only 121 innings in 2010.)
  • December 3, 2010: Jorge signed with the Rockies, receiving a two-year, $21.5 million contract, which includes a player option for 2013 that could raise its value to $32.5 million. And the Rockies also gets a club option for the 2014 as part of the deal that could take it to $43 million.
PITCHING:
  • The lefthanded de la Rosa has been compared to the Braves' John Rocker, as far as stuff and body size. Jorge fires in the 90-97 mph range with his four-seam FASTBALL. He also has a hard wicked SLIDER, a plus sweeping CURVEBALL with some depth, an occasional CUTTER, and a nasty 75-77 mph CHANGEUP that moves in either direction, depending on his release point.

    In 2004, De La Rosa added a two-seam fastball that has good sink.
  • Once a starter, Jorge is able to better exploit his ability out of the bullpen. He comes in and closes the door.
  • When he keeps his delivery together, he is a very good pitcher. He needs better command, though. And he needs to eliminate bad body language on the mound. De La Rosa will show his emotions out there at times. But Brewer pitching coach Mike Maddux straightened Jorge out in 2005, eliminating the sulky demeanor.

    But in 2007, De La Rosa was overthrowing, a pitcher that Royals manager Buddy Bell called "overcompetitive." When he starts overthrowing, he starts pitching away from contact and starts falling behind in the count.

    "It's not always stuff," Royals pitching coach Bob McClure said. "It's competing and knowing yourself. It's knowing what you can and can't do. It's knowing your limitations and actually really believing that you are better than whoever is up there, no matter who it is.
 "What happens with Jorge is he needs to get practicing thinking one pitch at a time and not get too far ahead of himself so the game doesn't speed up for him. If you just go one pitch at a time, the game starts to slow down."
  • Jorge has a pretty good feel for the pitching craft. He and Dodgers legend Fernando Valenzuela were teammates with Hermosillo of the Mexican League in 2001 and 2002. And Jorge listened to Fernando intently.

    "I learned a lot from him," De La Rosa said. "Every question I asked, he answered. He pitched for a long time and he knows a lot."
  • In 2009, De la Rosa had a remarkable turnaround. He was 0-6 in his first 10 starts, but after June 1 he went 16-3—the most wins in the Majors in that span. According to the Elias Sports Bureau, De La Rosa is only the third pitcher in Major League history, and the first since at least 1900, to win at least 16 games in a season in which he was 0-6 or worse through May 31.
  • In 2010, Jorge put up strong numbers in the second half of the year—opponents scored more than three earned runs off him only once in his final 14 starts.

    BREAKDOWN VS. LEFTIES AND RIGHTIES
  • In 2005, this righty pitcher allowed a .321 average, but no home runs in 56 at-bats to lefthanded hitters. And a .273 average with one homer in 110 at-bats to righthanded batters.

    In 2006, Jorge allowed a .250 average and 4 home runs in 60 at-bats vs. lefthanded batters. And a .269 mark with 10 home runs in 245 at-bats to righthanded hitters.

    In 2007, De La Rosa held lefthanded batters to a .234 average with one home run in 107 at-bats. But righthanded hitters had a .321 average with 19 home runs in 420 at-bats.

    In 2008, Jorge allowed a .289 average with 4 home runs in 121 at-bats. Righthanded batters had a .253 average with 9 home runs in 368 at-bats.

    In 2009, De La Rosa held lefthanded batters to a .204 average with 2 home runs and 152 at-bats. Righthanded hitters had a .262 average with 18 home runs in 538 at-bats.

    In 2010 Jorge held lefty hitters to a .206 average with 3 homers in 97 at-bats. Righthanded hitters had a .244 average with 12 home runs in 349 at-bats.
  • As of the start of the 2011 season, De La Rosa had a career record of 49-47 with a 5.02 ERA, having allowed 84 home runs and 723 hits in 711 innings.
CAREER INJURY REPORT:
  • March 13, 2004: De La Rosa injured his left groin during a spring training exhibition game and missed several days of action.
  • 2004 Season: Jorge had a sore elbow that caused him to lose command of his pitches. He was eventually shut down for a month.
  • June 10, 2006: De La Rosa was on the D.L with blisters on the middle finger of his left hand.
  • August 1–September 11, 2007: Jorge was on the D.L. with a strained left elbow.
  • October 2009: De La Rosa was left off the Rockies' post-season roster because of a strained left groin.
  • April 26-July 9, 2010: Jorge went on the D.L. with a torn tendon band in the middle finger of his throwing hand. He suffered it throwing a pitch early in a game he won vs. the Marlins, leaving after five innings because of numbness in the finger.
  • May 24, 2011: De La Rosa was on the D.L. and was lost for the season because of a complete tear of the unlar collateral ligament. Tommy John tendon replacement surgery was required.

    He began the 2012 season on the D    .L. while still recovering from the Tommy John surgery.

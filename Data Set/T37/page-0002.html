PERSONAL:
  • Knoedler bring a lot of heart and hustle to a team.
  • Justin's twin brother, Jason, is an outfielder in the Tigers organization. They were teammates at Miami of Ohio.
  • Justin originally signed with the Giants as a pitcher, converting to the catcher postion his first year, 2001. Steve Arnieri is the scout who signed Knoedler for the Giants.
  • Knoedler likes to spend all of his time working at the game. Even if you tell him to take a day off, he will be in the batting cage longer than anyone else.
  • Before 2005 spring training, the Baseball America Prospect Handbook rated Justin as 16th-best prospect in the Giants' organization. And in the spring of 2006, he was rated #26 by the book.
  • November, 2007: Knoedler signed with the A's organization.
  • November, 2008: Justin signed with the Cardinals' organization.
  • August, 2009: Knoelder sigend with the White Sox organization.
  • February 1, 2010: Justin signed with the Dodgers organization.
BATTING:
  • Knoedler (pronounced: Kuh-NAID-ler) is a tireless worker. He works hard at all aspects of the game.
  • Entering the 2006 season, Justin's career minor league batting average was .266, with 28 home runs in 1,340 at-bats.
  • Justin is not a big help with the bat. His glove is his best attribute. He doesn't have a lot of discipline at the plate, so Major League pitchers take advantage of that.
FIELDING:
  • He is a solid catcher and a popular teammate. Knoedler is known as "Dad" to his teammates.
  • It is his defense that can keep him in the majors as a backup catcher. He calls a great game.
  • Justin has a very good arm. He can throw out baserunners.

    In 2005, Knoedler threw out 38 percent of basestealers in the Pacific Coast League.
CAREER INJURY REPORT:
  • 2001: A broken hamate bone in his left hand kept Knoedler from catching his last year at Miami of Ohio. That inspired a conversion to the mound. Then he had surgery after the 2001 season.

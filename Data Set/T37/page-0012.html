PERSONAL:
  • Connor played mostly 3rd base at Birmingham-Southern, where he was the NAIA World Series MVP in 2002.

    He went on to become the school's all-time leader in home runs (60) and RBI (239) He only pitched six innings his first years of college, then became the closer for Birmingham-Southern in 2004, his senior year, and tied the school record with nine saves.
  • Robertson signed with the A's, and scout Steve Barningham, in 2004, after the team drafted him in the 31st round.
  • Robertson led the Arizona Rookie League in saves in 2004. And he had a 46-8 strikeout-to-walk ratio.
  • Before 2006 spring training, the Baseball America Prospect Handbook rated Connor as 24th-best prospect in the A's organization.
  • December 14, 2007: The Diamondbacks sent pitchers Brett Anderson, Dana Eveland and Greg Smith, outfielders Aaron Cunningham and Carlos Gonzalez and infielder Chris Carter to the A's, acquring Robertson and P Dan Haren.
  • December 12, 2008: The Mets sent P Scott Schoeneweis and cash to the Diamondbacks, acquiring Robertson.
  • October, 2009: Connor chose to become a free agent.
PITCHING:
  • Connor has an 88-92 mph sinking 2-seam FASTBALL, a CUTTER that he throws to the inside on righthanded hitters, keeping them honest, and a decent SLIDER. His pitches are hard for hitters to pick up. His cut fastball is also a very good pitch to retire lefthanded batters.
  • Robertson turns to the side in his delivery, all but completely blocking his arm from the batter's view. He has a lightning-quick release that just adds to his deception.
CAREER INJURY REPORT:
  • May 21-July 16, 2007: Robertson's right thumb was broken when struck by a batted ball during a game, so he went on the D.L.
  • Late in August, 2009: Connor was on the D.L. for a week or so, just before the end of the season.

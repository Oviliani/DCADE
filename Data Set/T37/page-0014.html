PERSONAL:
  • Barratt signed with the Rays, via scout Ricky Drexler, August 25, 2003, nearly 3 months after the team took him in the 5th round of the draft. He got a bonus of $300,000.

    Jonathan has spent the summer of 2003 pitching as the ace of the U.S. junior national team that won a silver medal at the Pan America Cup in Curacao.
  • Before 2005 spring training, the Baseball America Prospect Handbook rated Barratt as the 21st-best prospect in the Rays' organization. He wasn't rated in 2006, but in the spring of 2007, the book had Jonathan as #24 in the Tampa Bay farm system.
PITCHING:
  • Barratt is not your typical little lefty. He is very little, generously listed as 5-foot-9, but he can throw "lights out" stuff.
  • Jon has a moving 88-93 mph FASTBALL and a superb CURVEBALL. His CHANGEUP has good depth. And he shows a decent SPLIT-FINGER fastball.

    That hard curve of Barratt's is one of the best lefthanded curveballs in the Rays' farm system.
  • Barratt throws effortlessly from an incredibly quick left arm with exceptional mechanics. His offspeed pitches come from various arm angles.
  • Jonathan has very good control.
  • July 18, 2006: Barratt was named California League Pitcher of the Week. In two starts for A Visalia, Jon went 1-0 with a 1.42 ERA, striking out 13 in those starts.
  • He is a scrappy lefthander -- tough and resilient.
CAREER INJURY REPORT:
  • April, 2004: A sore arm left Barratt in extended spring training, but he rebounded.
  • August, 2004: Jonathan suffered a strained back.
  • April-May, 2007: Barratt started the season on the D.L. He was reactivated the end of the first week of May.
